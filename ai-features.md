# AI Features for Future of Work

**Prepared by:** Engineering
**Date:** 29 May 2026
**For:** Internal review

---

### 1. The Sierra Leone Talent Graph

**What it is.** A living, real-time map of every skill, submission, win, earning event, and credential on the platform — maintained automatically by AI as freelancers work.

**What it does.** Anyone with permission can query it for instant answers: where the top 100 React developers are, what skills women are gaining fastest, where there is a skill surplus or shortage, which universities produce the most platform-ready graduates. As more people work on FOW, the graph becomes richer.

**Why it matters.** Sierra Leone has no labour-market data. MOCTI cannot plan for the 65,000 technology jobs without knowing what skills exist where. Universities cannot update curricula without knowing what the market actually pays for. Funders cannot measure impact without trustworthy numbers. FOW becomes the country's first real-time labour-market data infrastructure — and the only one nobody else can build, because nobody else has the work-event data we will have.

---

### 2. Global Workforce Brokerage

**What it is.** An AI agent that hunts global opportunities on behalf of our freelancers, instead of waiting for global clients to discover Sierra Leone.

**What it does.** The agent continuously scans job posts on Upwork, LinkedIn, AngelList, Toptal, and similar platforms. It finds opportunities that match our freelancers' verified skills, assembles a curated team or individual from the FOW roster, and submits a proposal on their behalf — with portfolio, credentials, and price. The freelancer reviews and approves before anything is committed.

**Why it matters.** The Year 2 milestone is 200+ freelancers earning on global platforms. Today, that depends on each freelancer learning to write Upwork bids, optimise profiles, and chase clients across time zones — most won't. With this agent, FOW does the hunting for them. We stop waiting for the global gig economy to find Sierra Leone and start exporting Sierra Leonean talent into it by default. This is the feature that turns the 200+ target into 2,000+.

## Part 2 — Supporting AI features

### For freelancers

**3. Submission Coach**
_Who it's for:_ Freelancers about to submit work.
_What it does:_ Before a freelancer hits submit, AI checks their work against the bounty brief and flags gaps — "you didn't address requirement 3," "your design is missing the mobile view they asked for." It does not grade. It coaches.
_Why it matters:_ Most submissions lose for fixable reasons. This raises win rates and submission quality across the platform.

**4. Loss Feedback Generator**
_Who it's for:_ Freelancers who didn't win.
_What it does:_ When a freelancer loses, AI writes personalised feedback explaining what was missing compared to the winning submission. Sponsors rarely write feedback themselves — AI does it from the winner and the brief.
_Why it matters:_ Turns every loss into a learning moment. Keeps freelancers engaged instead of leaving the platform after losses.

**5. Earning Strategy Planner**
_Who it's for:_ Freelancers planning their next month of work.
_What it does:_ Based on the freelancer's skills, history, and goals, AI builds a 4-week earning plan — which bounties to target, in what order, for what total income. An agentic version runs continuously and surfaces matched opportunities each morning.
_Why it matters:_ Most freelancers waste time chasing the wrong work. This treats each freelancer like a portfolio manager of their own time.

**6. Skill Gap Analyser**
_Who it's for:_ Freelancers planning their growth.
_What it does:_ Looks at bounties the freelancer is missing out on and identifies the specific skill that would unlock them — "Learn this one form library and you qualify for 12 more bounties paying $X on average."
_Why it matters:_ Replaces vague upskilling ("learn React") with a concrete, paid-work-driven growth path.

### For sponsors and businesses

**7. Bounty Brief Builder**
_Who it's for:_ Sponsors, especially small businesses new to digital work.
_What it does:_ The sponsor types 1–2 sentences about what they need. AI drafts the full bounty — deliverables, requirements, acceptance criteria, suggested prize, suggested deadline. The sponsor edits and posts.
_Why it matters:_ 84% of Sierra Leonean businesses have no digital presence. They cannot write a good brief. Bad briefs produce bad submissions. This fixes the root problem.

**8. Digital Transformation Diagnostic**
_Who it's for:_ Small businesses joining the platform for the first time.
_What it does:_ An AI-led onboarding conversation that asks about the business's operations and suggests which parts could become bounties — "you mentioned you make flyers manually every month; that's a recurring bounty." Each business leaves with a personalised plan for using FOW.
_Why it matters:_ Directly serves the Digital Transformation Engine pillar of the funding pitch. Businesses don't just sign up — they leave with a roadmap.

**9. Bounty Splitter Agent**
_Who it's for:_ Sponsors with bigger projects.
_What it does:_ Sponsor says "I need a website." The agent decomposes that into smaller bounties (logo, content, design, build, QA), suggests prize allocation for each, sequences them so outputs from one feed the next, and coordinates handoffs between the freelancers working on each piece.
_Why it matters:_ Lets non-technical sponsors post complex projects without knowing how to break the work down. Unlocks larger projects the platform could not otherwise serve.

### For platform trust

**10. Submission Provenance Verifier**
_Who it's for:_ The platform itself and sponsors who pay for human work.
_What it does:_ Uses signals from version control, design tool history, browser sessions, and behavioural patterns to prove that a submission was genuinely produced by the claimed human, not scraped or AI-generated without disclosure.
_Why it matters:_ Across the global gig economy, bounty platforms are racing to _allow_ AI agents to compete with humans for work. FOW takes the opposite position: every submission cryptographically verified as human-produced. This becomes a competitive differentiator and protects our entire employment mission. Without it, AI farms could flood the platform and crowd out the youth we are here to employ.

### For platform growth

**11. Informal Channel Demand Aggregator**
_Who it's for:_ The platform's growth team.
_What it does:_ AI monitors public hiring posts on WhatsApp groups and Facebook pages (which is how nearly all Sierra Leonean businesses currently hire). When it spots a real job, it drafts an FOW bounty from the post and prompts the growth team to reach out to the poster with a ready-to-publish version: "we already turned your post into a bounty — approve and we'll launch it."
_Why it matters:_ Our actual competitor is informal networks, not other platforms. This feature attacks the informal hiring market directly, at scale, with no manual outreach.

## Strategic angle (for the conversation upward)

These features sit in three layers:

1. **Talent Graph and Workforce Brokerage** are platform-defining. They are the reason a funder, the World Bank, or MOCTI takes a meeting. They reframe FOW from "a bounty platform" to "national labour-market infrastructure with an export pipeline into the global gig economy."

2. **Provenance Verifier** is positioning. While the rest of the industry races to let AI agents take freelance work, FOW is engineered to protect human employment outcomes. This is a brand pillar, not just a feature.

3. **The other eight features** are the everyday AI that makes the platform smarter for freelancers and sponsors. Each one solves a specific friction point we've already seen in the pilot.

Built together, these features are not a checklist of AI we are adding — they are the operating system of the next version of Future of Work.
